About Us

Cafena opened in St. Mark's Square, where it remains today, on December 29, 1720.

Sitting comfortably in the magnificent halls, completely restored in the mid-19th century, you can breathe in the city's long and colorful history that unfolded outside the café's stained-glass windows: the rise and fall of the Venetian Republic, conspirators plotting the end of French and, later, Austrian rule — in fact, the wounded from the 1848 revolt were treated inside its halls.

The café remains open and active even in times of war, continuing to serve as the ideal setting for a light meal, refreshments and meeting with friends.
